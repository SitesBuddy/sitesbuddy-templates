# Local Alternatives — Template Customisation Guide

This is a marketplace or shop template for local, artisan, and independently made goods. It's warm and community-focused, designed to showcase local makers and their products.

## Key things to customise

**Brand name**
- Replace "Made Here." with the actual marketplace or shop name throughout
- Update the nav tagline and any instances where the brand is mentioned

**Hero**
- Update the 3 stats (currently 64 makers, 380+ products, 100% county-made) to real numbers
- Adjust the tagline to reflect the specific geography or focus (e.g. "100% county Wicklow made")

**Product grid**
- Replace all 6 product cards with real products
- Each card has: emoji image, maker name, product name, description, price, and a tag (Local / Handmade / Seasonal)
- The coloured product image backgrounds are CSS classes — adjust the gradient colours if needed

**Category filter pills**
- Update the category labels to match the actual product categories stocked
- These are currently cosmetic (no JS filtering) — a developer can add filtering logic if required

**Maker spotlight**
- Replace the featured maker section with a real maker story
- Update the emoji photo, name, description, and quote
- Change the "Shop Claire's honey" button link to the relevant product anchor

**Why buy local section**
- The 4 "why" cards can be edited or removed — keep whichever reasons resonate with this shop's audience

**Values strip**
- Change the 4 commitment badges to reflect actual commitments (e.g. change "50km" radius if different)

**Email signup CTA**
- The form at the bottom is cosmetic — wire up to a mailing list provider (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)

**Colours**
- `--earth: #3d2b1a` — rich dark brown (nav, hero, CTA)
- `--amber: #c8791c` — warm amber (accents, buttons)
- `--sage: #3e6b4a` — forest green (values strip)
- `--cream: #faf7f1` — warm off-white (body background)

## What this template works for
- Buy-local marketplace or directory
- Artisan food shop or deli
- Craft goods or gifts shop
- Farmers market with an online presence
- County food brand or local co-operative
